# Aspects Reference

An aspect is an angular relationship between two planets. It describes how their energies interact.

## Major Aspects

| Aspect | Symbol | Angle | Orb | Nature | Keyword |
|--------|--------|-------|-----|--------|---------|
| Conjunction | ☌ | 0° | ±8° | Variable | Fusion, intensification |
| Sextile | ⚹ | 60° | ±4° | Harmonious | Opportunity, ease |
| Square | □ | 90° | ±8° | Tense | Friction, growth through challenge |
| Trine | △ | 120° | ±8° | Harmonious | Flow, natural talent |
| Opposition | ☍ | 180° | ±8° | Tense | Polarity, awareness through contrast |

## Aspect Interpretations

### ☌ Conjunction (0°)
Two planets merge their energies. The result depends on the planets involved.
- **Benefic + Benefic** (e.g., Venus–Jupiter): amplified good fortune, potential for excess
- **Malefic + Malefic** (e.g., Mars–Saturn): intensified difficulty, but also power when mastered
- **Benefic + Malefic** (e.g., Venus–Mars): magnetic tension; passion or conflict
- The conjunction is the most powerful aspect — neither planet functions independently

### ⚹ Sextile (60°)
Complementary energies that support each other. Requires slight effort to activate — not as effortless as a trine. Good for networking, learning, and creative collaboration.

### □ Square (90°)
Two planets at cross-purposes. The tension demands action; avoidance breeds frustration. Squares are among the most developmental aspects — they build character and achievement through difficulty.
- Creates urgency and ambition
- The challenge is learning to integrate both energies rather than fighting oneself

### △ Trine (120°)
Planets in the same element flowing naturally together. Often points to natural talents that are so easy they can be taken for granted.
- Can lead to complacency
- In a reading, trines show what comes effortlessly; remind the person to actively use these gifts

### ☍ Opposition (180°)
Two planets in opposing signs. Creates awareness through projection — the person may see the opposing planet's qualities in others rather than themselves.
- Points to the need for balance and integration
- Often shows up in relationships: we attract the "other side"
- The most relational of the major aspects

---

## Applying vs. Separating

- **Applying** (orb decreasing, planets moving toward exact): the aspect is building in strength; its themes are emerging
- **Separating** (orb increasing, planets moving apart): the event or theme has peaked; integrating the lesson

---

## Orb Guidelines

Tighter orbs = stronger, more conscious manifestation. Looser orbs = subtler, background influence.

| Planets | Suggested Orb |
|---------|--------------|
| Sun or Moon involved | up to 10° for major aspects |
| Two personal planets | up to 8° |
| Personal + outer planet | up to 5° |
| Two outer planets | up to 3° |

---

## Node and Chiron Contacts

These contacts can outweigh ordinary aspects when tight.

- **Planet conjunct North Node**: the planet becomes part of the life direction; growth requires conscious development of that function
- **Planet conjunct South Node**: the planet shows an old talent or defense pattern that is easy to fall back on
- **Planet square the Nodes**: a skipped-step pattern; the planet's issue keeps interrupting forward movement until integrated
- **Chiron conjunct / square / oppose personal planets or angles**: the wound is personalized and visible in daily life
- **Chiron trine / sextile**: healing capacity is real, but can be underused unless named directly

Use karmic language carefully: symbolic, developmental, and chart-grounded.

## Key Planetary Pairs — Interpretive Shortcuts

| Pair | Harmonious (trine/sextile) | Tense (square/opposition) |
|------|---------------------------|--------------------------|
| Sun–Moon | Inner unity; emotions support purpose | Tension between will and need; parents may have clashed |
| Sun–Venus | Charm, social ease, creative expression | Vanity, approval-seeking |
| Sun–Mars | Drive and purpose aligned; vitality | Combativeness, impatience |
| Moon–Venus | Emotional warmth; affectionate | Indulgence, passive-aggressive |
| Moon–Saturn | Emotional discipline; maturity | Emotional restriction; fear of vulnerability |
| Venus–Mars | Passionate harmony; attraction | Push-pull in relationships; desire vs. love |
| Jupiter–Saturn | Disciplined expansion; timing | Boom-bust cycles; restriction vs. growth |
| Mars–Saturn | Determined perseverance | Frustration, blocked action |
| Sun–Saturn | Authority, achievement, father themes | Harsh inner critic; delayed success |
