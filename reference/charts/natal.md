# Natal Chart Reading Framework

A natal chart is a snapshot of the sky at the moment and place of birth. It describes core personality, life themes, strengths, and developmental edges.

## Reading Sequence

Work through these layers in order. Not every layer needs equal coverage — focus where the chart speaks loudest.

---

### Layer 1 — Chart Shape (Overview)

Before planets, notice the overall pattern:

| Shape | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Bundle** | All planets in ~120° arc — focus and specialization; blind spots in empty areas |
| **Bowl** | All planets in one hemisphere — self-contained; driven by what is lacking |
| **Bucket** | All planets in one hemisphere + one singleton opposite — the singleton planet is the "handle"; channels all energy |
| **Locomotive** | All planets in ~240° arc with ~120° empty — strong drive; pioneering |
| **Seesaw** | Two opposing clusters — tension between two life areas; seeks balance |
| **Splay** | Three or more evenly spaced clusters — multi-talented; individualistic |
| **Splash** | Planets spread across all signs — varied experience; interest in everything |

Also note:
- **Hemisphere emphasis**: above the horizon (public life) vs. below (private); east (independent/self-made) vs. west (relational/other-focused)

---

### Layer 2 — The Big Three

These three points define the core of the personality.

1. **Sun sign** — the conscious self, identity, life purpose, where to shine (see `signs.md`)
2. **Moon sign** — emotional needs, instincts, the inner world, comfort zones (see `signs.md`)
3. **Ascendant (Rising sign)** — the social mask, physical presence, how the world first sees them (cusp of 1st house)

Note the element balance among the Big Three. All in fire = high energy. Mixed = more nuanced blend.

---

### Layer 3 — Chart Ruler

The ruling planet of the Ascendant sign is the **chart ruler**. It is especially important — its sign, house, and aspects color the entire chart.

| Ascendant | Chart Ruler |
|-----------|-------------|
| Aries | Mars |
| Taurus | Venus |
| Gemini | Mercury |
| Cancer | Moon |
| Leo | Sun |
| Virgo | Mercury |
| Libra | Venus |
| Scorpio | Pluto (Mars) |
| Sagittarius | Jupiter |
| Capricorn | Saturn |
| Aquarius | Uranus (Saturn) |
| Pisces | Neptune (Jupiter) |

Describe: "Your chart ruler is [planet] in [sign] in the [Nth] house, which means..."

---

### Layer 4 — Dominant Element & Mode

Count planets (Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto) in each element and mode.

**Element balance:**
- Fire dominant: initiative, but may lack groundedness or emotional sensitivity
- Earth dominant: practical, but may lack spontaneity or vision
- Air dominant: intellectual, but may lack emotional depth
- Water dominant: intuitive, but may lack structure
- **Missing element**: a blind spot; often shows up as an overcompensation or fascination

**Mode balance:**
- Cardinal heavy: action-oriented, starts many things
- Fixed heavy: determined but resistant to change
- Mutable heavy: highly adaptable but may lack follow-through

---

### Layer 5 — Planets in Houses

Identify which houses are occupied. Angular houses (1, 4, 7, 10) carry the most public weight.

For each occupied house:
1. Name the planet(s)
2. Describe the life area (see `houses.md`)
3. Apply planet-in-sign meaning (see `planets.md`)
4. Note any aspects from that planet (see `aspects.md`)

Pay special attention to:
- **Stellium** (3+ planets in one house): dominant life focus
- **Empty 7th house**: partnerships flow more naturally; look to ruler for how
- **12th house planets**: hidden strengths or unconscious patterns

Read loaded houses as **psychic geography**, not as separate rooms. The 4th/8th/12th store the inherited and hidden material; when they are heavy, the chart's brightest house is usually the flight path away from them, and Saturn's house is where the rebuilding is demanded. Chain these into the chart's single causal story — inheritance, flight, refuge, cost, repetition, exit — before interpreting any single placement. (See "Before writing: diagnose the chart" in `reference/delivery.md`.)

Then cash the geometry out into **concrete people and domains** — mother, father, spouse, money, inheritance, health — using the significator method and complex table in `life_domains.md`. The houses are not abstractions: the 4th contains actual parents, the 7th an actual future spouse, the 2nd/8th actual money.

---

### Layer 6 — Major Aspects

Focus on aspects with tight orbs (under 3°), then expand to standard orbs.

Priority aspects:
1. Sun aspects — core identity challenges or supports
2. Moon aspects — emotional patterns
3. Venus–Mars aspects — relationship and desire dynamics
4. Saturn aspects — life lessons, discipline
5. Outer planet aspects to personal planets (Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars) — generational influence made personal

Use the aspect pair table in `aspects.md` for interpretive shortcuts.

---

### Layer 7 — Evolutionary Axis & Core Wound

After the standard planets and aspects, read the chart's deeper developmental layer:

1. **North Node / South Node** — the familiar pattern vs. the growth direction.
2. **Chiron** — the core wound, defensive adaptation, and healing intelligence.
3. **Node or Chiron contacts** under 3° to personal planets or angles — elevate these above ordinary aspects.

Use language like:
- "an old pattern"
- "a familiar survival strategy"
- "the direction this life keeps pushing you toward"
- "the lesson becomes real through repeated choices"
- "this pattern is strong, but not final"

Do **not** state literal past-life facts as certainty.
Do **not** write as if the chart removes agency. Always distinguish pattern from choice.

---

### Layer 8 — Asteroid Chapter

If the chart includes supported asteroids or minor bodies, gather them into one dedicated chapter.

1. Start with the **2-4 most meaningful bodies**, not a flat list.
2. Prioritize:
   - angular houses
   - 5th / 7th / 8th / 11th / 12th houses
   - conjunctions, oppositions, squares, trines, sextiles, or quincunxes within about 3°
3. Translate them into lived themes, not catalog keywords.

If a tight aspect exists, read the asteroid through that contact:
- `Juno + Venus/Mars/Saturn` → commitment pattern
- `Ceres + Moon/Venus/Saturn` → attachment / nourishment pattern
- `Vesta + Sun/Mars/ASC` → devotion / self-containment pattern
- `Pallas + Mercury/Saturn/MC` → strategy / pattern recognition pattern
- outer minor bodies (Eris, Sedna, Haumea, Makemake, Ixion, Orcus, Quaoar, Pholus) → only when house placement or exact aspect clearly makes them relevant

---

### Layer 9 — Synthesis & Life Themes

Tie layers together into 2–4 key themes. Example structure:

> "Your chart emphasizes [element/mode], centered around [houses]. The Sun in [sign] in the [Nth] house, combined with [key aspect], suggests a life path around [theme]. Your Moon in [sign] points to a need for [emotional need], while [chart ruler] in [house] shapes how this all manifests."

**Finish with:**
- Core gifts (what the chart does naturally well)
- Key developmental edges (where growth asks more)
- An affirming statement connecting the two
- One concrete freedom point: the response that changes how the chart is lived


## Common Patterns Worth Naming

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Sun square Moon | Inner tension between ego-drives and emotional needs; parents had conflicting styles |
| Venus–Saturn aspect | Love requires effort; delayed or conditional relationships; deepens with age |
| Mars–Pluto aspect | Intense drive; power struggles; enormous capacity when focused |
| Moon in 12th | Hidden emotional world; needs solitude to process feelings; strong empathy |
| Stellium in 8th/12th | Depth, intensity, or spirituality central to identity |
| All planets below horizon | Inner-focused; private; powerful when directed outward |
| Grand trine | Extraordinary ease in one element; must avoid stagnation |
| T-square | Dynamic tension seeking resolution; the empty leg is the outlet |
| Grand cross | Four-way tension; intense pressure that forges strength |
| Yod ("Finger of God") | Two sextile planets both quincunx a third; fated adjustment point |
| Planet conjunct North Node | The growth path becomes unmistakable; that planet's function must mature |
| Planet conjunct South Node | A familiar gift or reflex is over-learned; growth requires loosening identification |
| Chiron conjunct Sun/Moon/ASC | The wound is central to identity, emotional safety, or self-presentation |
