# Synastry & Composite Chart Reading Framework

Use this reference for both **synastry** (planet-to-planet contacts between two charts) and **composite** (midpoint chart representing the relationship as an entity).

---

## Part 1 — Synastry

Synastry overlays two charts to reveal how the individuals interact, where they support each other, and where friction arises.

### Grounding

Ground the synastry reading in:

1. both natal charts,
2. the cross-chart contacts from `script/synastry_raw_chart.py` output,
3. optional retrieval for the dominant bond pattern (see `reference/retrieval_protocol.md`).

Do **not** show numeric compatibility scores, percentages, star levels, or grades.
Do **not** write as if a karmic bond removes choice. Synastry shows pressure, attraction, and lesson structure; people still decide whether and how to live it.

### Before writing: diagnose the bond (internal work, never shown)

A relationship reading answers four questions — *why did these two find each other, what does the bond run on, where does it break, and what is each person supposed to learn* — never "are they compatible". Work this out privately first:

1. **Each natal spine first.** You cannot read a synastry without knowing each person's curriculum. Derive each chart's one-sentence spine (main conflict + karmic axis, per the diagnosis protocol in `reference/delivery.md`) before looking at a single cross-aspect. The relationship's story is written where the two spines interlock.

2. **The hook.** Take the 2-4 tightest cross-contacts and read them as the attraction mechanism. The hook is almost always unfinished business: each person's planets landing on the other's wound and karmic points (Moon, Venus, Saturn, Juno, Nodes, angles). We hire partners to replay the original wound with a chance of a different ending — say *whose* pattern recognizes *whose*, specifically. If the hook were merely pleasant, they would be friends; name what makes it magnetic.

3. **The cycle.** Chain the cross-aspects into the bond's repeating loop: what the honeymoon runs on (the tightest harmonious contacts), what friction it converts into (the hard contacts, as consequences — "and so...", not "also..."), what the recurring crisis looks like, and how the loop restarts. House overlays say *where* it plays out (his Saturn in her 2nd = the weight lands on money; her Mars in his 12th = her fire hits his hidden room). When independent signatures converge on a concrete outcome — power struggle, disillusionment, triangle, money conflict — name it plainly as the pattern's price, never as fate.

4. **The two curricula.** Cross-contacts to Nodes, Saturn, and Chiron mark the karmic teachers. A relationship is a school two souls co-enroll in, but **they are never taking the same course** — state A's lesson and B's lesson separately.

5. **The graduation equipment.** Match the bond's stabilizing resources (tight harmonious cross-contacts, dignified planets meeting, the composite's strengths) to each specific difficulty, and name the choice-point each person owns. A bond survives on named agreements, not on chemistry.

### Output structure (relationship readings)

1. **Bond portrait** — one paragraph telling the relationship's whole story: why they met, what it runs on, where it fights, what it is for
2. **Why you found each other** — the hook, traced from each person's natal spine
3. **The cycle** — honeymoon mechanism → friction → the recurring crisis, cashed into concrete arenas (money, home, sex, in-laws, work)
4. **Two curricula** — what A is here to learn, what B is here to learn (separately)
5. **The relationship itself** — composite: the entity's purpose and pressure point
6. **Graduation** — each difficulty paired with the resource and the agreement that holds it
7. **The opening question** — end the first turn by *locating* the client's real question (see "Answering the real question" below): ask which of the four it is rather than guessing. In subsequent turns, answer it with conditional framing, cost pricing, typology, or the 正缘 profile — never a verdict, never "优势与挑战并存" mush. Run the consultation loop per `reference/delivery.md`.

The contact tables below are the evidence library for this diagnosis — work through them for coverage, but never output them as a list.

### Answering the real question (合不合适)

Clients almost never mean "are we compatible" literally. The question is a disguise for one of four real questions — **identify which one before writing the closing**, because the same synastry data answers each differently:

1. **"我该不该离开"** — already leaning out, seeking permission. Giving them repair techniques is torture.
2. **"他/她还爱我吗、是什么样的人"** — information anxiety about the partner. Wants a window, needs a mirror.
3. **"我们的问题还能不能修"** — leaning in, seeking method and hope. Giving them "no future" is cruelty.
4. **"这是不是我的正缘"** — not asking about this person at all; asking about their own fate.

**Never deliver a verdict** ("you're incompatible / you should break up"). Three reasons: birth-time uncertainty makes verdict-level confidence technically dishonest; verdicts create self-fulfilling prophecies and dependency; and a conclusion the client reaches themselves is a hundred times more durable than one they are handed. The craft is to hold up a mirror clear enough that the client reads the answer in it.

**Four veiled techniques** (for relationships the chart shows as unworkable, use these — never the blunt verdict, never the mush of "优势与挑战并存"):

- **Conditional framing**: state the exact conditions under which the bond survives — "this works if he becomes someone who can voice anger in the moment, AND you become someone who doesn't need constant proof of love; both are ten-year curricula, and one person changing is not enough." The more specific the conditions, the clearer the client's own scale reads. The listener completes the conclusion.
- **Cost pricing**: don't advise leaving; itemize the price of staying — the recurring cycle, the career concessions, the financial exposure — then hand over the bill: "if this price is one you can pay and want to pay, stay." Unassailable, because you never advised anything; you just refused to discount.
- **Relationship typology**: distinguish **curriculum relationships** (the hook lands entirely on old wounds; the bond's gravity points at both South Nodes — it came to teach) from **companion relationships**. For a curriculum bond: "this relationship's role in your life is a teacher, not a home. Teachers leave when the course is done — and the better the course goes, the more natural the leaving. The question isn't whether you two fit; it's whether you've finished the course." This reframes the ending as graduation, not failure, and leaves the timing with the client. It is the ceiling of the veiled exit.
- **Mirroring**: describe the couple's loop in forensic detail down to the sixth week after the reconciliation. When the description is precise enough to chill, no advice is needed — recognition itself loosens the pattern.

**Speaking of 正缘** (the destined/right partnership):

- **The profile method** (most elegant): the natal 7th house, its ruler, Venus and Juno give the *spec sheet* of this person's durable partnership — qualities, meeting context, maturity timing. Paint the full profile **without mentioning the current partner once**. The client compares silently: a match is reassurance, a mismatch is the gentlest diagnosis ever written — and no verdict was uttered.
- **The time window**: transits give seasons, not deliveries — "your 7th house is activated in [period]: existing bonds get asked for an answer, new ones arrive carrying weight. A window is a planting season, not a delivery date."
- **Conditionalize 正缘**: the most honest layer — the right relationship is not a person fate ships, but the relationship the client becomes able to receive after their curriculum progresses. Bind 正缘 to the natal karmic axis: "yours appears after you can say 'I want' to someone you love — before that, whoever arrives gets processed by the old pattern. It's not that they haven't come; it's that they weren't recognizable." This turns waiting into work and the client from passenger into driver.

**Red lines** (where veiling stops):

1. **Safety exception**: when converging signatures suggest escalating control or violence AND the client's account corroborates — be direct about safety. Tact here is malpractice.
2. **Never predict a third party's behavior as fact** ("he will cheat") — at most, name the structural conditions loyalty needs in this bond.
3. **No date guarantees** — "you'll marry in March 2027" is fortune-telling; "2026-27 is a defining window for your partnerships" is craft.
4. **No destiny-lock language** ("you're fated, bound across lifetimes") — however dramatic the chart, this manufactures dependency and paralysis.

The success criterion of a relationship reading: **the client leaves more able to decide than they arrived — never more dependent on the astrologer.**

### Reading Sequence for Synastry

#### Step 1 — Sun & Moon Contacts

| Contact | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| P1 Sun conjunct/trine/sextile P2 Moon | Natural emotional attunement; one partner's purpose feeds the other's needs |
| P1 Sun square/opposition P2 Moon | Tension between head and heart; each partner may frustrate the other unintentionally |
| P1 Moon conjunct P2 Moon | Shared emotional wavelength; comfortable and familiar |

#### Step 2 — Venus & Mars Contacts (Attraction Layer)

| Contact | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Venus conjunct/trine/sextile Mars | Strong attraction; desire and affection flow together |
| Venus square/opposition Mars | Magnetic but tense; passion mixed with conflict |
| Venus conjunct Venus | Shared values and aesthetic; natural pleasure in each other |
| Mars conjunct Mars | Amplified drive; can be competitive or powerfully aligned |

#### Step 3 — Ascendant & Descendant Contacts

| Contact | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| P1 planet conjunct P2 Ascendant | P1 strongly shapes how P2 presents to the world |
| P1 Sun/Moon conjunct P2 Descendant | P1 embodies what P2 seeks in a partner |

#### Step 4 — Saturn Contacts (Commitment & Karma)

| Contact | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Saturn conjunct Sun/Moon/Venus | Serious, long-lasting, possibly karmic connection; one may feel restricted by the other |
| Saturn trine/sextile personal planets | Stability, loyalty, durability |
| Saturn square/opposition personal planets | One partner imposes limits on the other; requires mutual respect |

#### Step 4.5 — Juno & asteroid contacts (contract / care / devotion layer)

| Contact | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Juno conjunct/trine/sextile Sun/Moon/Venus | Shared commitment instinct; the relationship naturally activates vows, reciprocity, and "what this bond means" |
| Juno square/opposition personal planets | Mismatch around promises, exclusivity, fairness, or expectations of partnership |
| Juno with Saturn / Nodes | Strong karmic-duty or formalization theme; can feel fated, binding, or heavy |
| Ceres contacts | How each person gives care, soothing, nourishment, and maternal / dependency patterns |
| Vesta contacts | Devotion, sacrifice, singular focus, and what must be protected in the bond |
| Pallas contacts | Strategic compatibility, pattern-recognition, and whether they solve problems as a team |

#### Step 5 — Outer Planet Contacts (Transformation)

| Contact | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Pluto–Venus/Mars | Intense, transformative, possibly obsessive attraction |
| Neptune–Venus/Moon | Romantic idealism; enchantment; possible illusion |
| Uranus–Sun/Moon | Exciting and unpredictable; freedom vs. commitment tension |

#### Step 6 — Synthesis

Summarize in three areas:
1. **Strengths**: What natural gifts and harmonies does this synastry offer?
2. **Challenges**: Where do the planets create friction or misunderstanding?
3. **Growth Areas**: What does this relationship invite each person to develop?
4. **Choice-point**: What each person must consciously choose, stop, or tolerate less of for the bond to become healthier

## Part 2 — Composite Chart

The composite chart is calculated from the midpoints of both charts. It describes the relationship itself — its purpose, personality, and potential.

### Reading the Composite as a Natal Chart

Apply the same layered approach as `natal.md` but frame everything in terms of "the relationship":

1. **Composite Ascendant**: How the relationship presents to the outside world
2. **Composite Sun**: The core purpose and identity of the relationship
3. **Composite Moon**: The emotional needs and inner life of the relationship
4. **Composite Venus**: How love, pleasure, and appreciation are shared
5. **Composite Mars**: How the relationship takes action and asserts itself
6. **Composite Saturn**: Where the relationship must work hardest; karmic purpose

### Relationship Themes

The `relationship_themes[]` array from the ephemeris output contains extracted composite chart themes. Use these as starting points, then expand with reference context.

### Key Composite Patterns

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Composite Sun in 7th/8th | The relationship is a central life event for both |
| Composite Moon in 4th | Relationship feels like home; domestic/family-oriented |
| Composite Saturn in 1st | Serious, structured; felt as a "heavy" relationship; long-lasting |
| Composite Venus–Jupiter | Expansive joy; generosity; can become indulgent |
| Composite Mars–Pluto | Intense power dynamic; transformation through conflict |
| Composite Neptune–Venus | Dreamy, spiritual love; may dissolve sense of individual self |

### Composite vs. Synastry

- **Synastry**: How Person A and Person B interact with *each other*
- **Composite**: What the *relationship itself* is

Both should ideally be read together. A difficult synastry with a strong composite can still build something lasting.


## Delivering the Reading

Use the **Output structure (relationship readings)** defined in Part 1 above — bond portrait, the hook, the cycle, two curricula, the composite entity, graduation. Close on the sentence that holds the bond's central paradox, never on a question or a score.
